Targets the root causes of aging,

not just superficial symptoms.

We target aging on a cellular level

Our products are designed to address the 14 scientifically proven root causes of ageing on a cellular level

  • 1.

    Genomic Instability: Accumulation of damage in DNA over time, leading to mutations and disruptions in cell function.

  • 2.

    Telomere Attrition: Shortening of telomeres, the protective ends of chromosomes, which leads to cellular ageing and malfunction.

  • 3.

    Epigenetic Alterations: Changes in gene expression regulation without altering the DNA sequence, affecting how genes are turned on or off.

  • 4.

    Loss of Proteostasis: Decline in the cell's ability to synthesize, fold, and degrade proteins properly, leading to the accumulation of damaged proteins.

  • 5.

    Deregulated Nutrient-Sensing: Disruption in the pathways that cells use to sense nutrients, affecting metabolism and energy production.

  • 6.

    Mitochondrial Dysfunction: Decrease in the efficiency of mitochondria, the cell's powerhouses, leading to reduced energy production and increased oxidative stress.

  • 7.

    Cellular Senescence: The process where cells lose their ability to divide and function properly, often contributing to inflammation and tissue dysfunction.

  • 8.

    Stem Cell Exhaustion: Reduction in the number and function of stem cells, impairing the body's ability to regenerate and repair tissues.

  • 9.

    Altered Intercellular Communication: Changes in the signals cells use to communicate, which can disrupt tissue function and contribute to chronic inflammation.

  • 10.

    Microbiome Disturbance: Imbalances in our body's microbial community can trigger health problems.

  • 11.

    Compromised Autophagy: As we age, the cellular clean-up crew, known as autophagy, becomes less efficient.

  • 12.

    Inflammation: Aging can make our body's inflammation response like an overly sensitive fire alarm, triggering false alarms and causing undue harm.

  • 13.

    Splicing Dysregulation: Gene splicing is genetic editing, which age can disrupt, causing gene malfunction.

  • 14.

    Altered Mechanical Properties: Aging can change our tissues' mechanical properties, like a well-used machine gradually losing its smooth operation.
